Context

Long-term climate and special extreme events evolution, such as tropical cyclones, are widly studied for more accurate predictions. In this context the estimates of sea spray behavior has revealed to be impactfull. Moreover in recent years, it has been suggested that these estimates need to incorporate an accurate description of the transport of sea spray within the atmospheric boundary layer and the drop-induced fluxes of momentum, heat and moisture, so that the resulting effects on atmospheric flow can be evaluated.

On the basis of Fabirce Veron's and Luc Mieussens's paper An Eulerian model for sea spray transport and evaporation, we numerically studied the Lagrangian and the Eulerian modele using different methodes detailed in the corresponding sections.

Theoretical Background

Here we present a brief theoretical foundation for the study:

The Navier-Stokes equations for incompressible flow are given by:

\[ \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u} = -\nabla p + \nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u}, \quad \nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0 \]

Where \( \mathbf{u} \) is the velocity field, \( p \) is the pressure, and \( \nu \) is the kinematic viscosity.
